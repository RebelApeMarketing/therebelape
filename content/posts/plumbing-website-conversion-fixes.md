<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Step 1: Put Your Phone Number in the Top Right Corner of EVERY Page</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Problem</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One of the most overlooked plumbing website conversion tactics is that many home service businesses unknowingly hide their phone number or only display it on the contact page. This is a critical mistake that's costing you leads every single day.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Why It Matters</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The top right corner of your website gets more attention than any other section in the header. Yet countless plumbing and home service websites fail to capitalize on this prime real estate.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For emergency services especially, this oversight is deadly to your business. When someone has a burst pipe or HVAC emergency, they're not going to hunt around your website looking for contact information. If they can't find your number immediately, they'll simply hit the back button and call your competitor instead.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even when your branding is strong, potential customers will often choose the competitor who makes it easier to get in touch. Convenience trumps brand loyalty, especially in emergency situations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Solution</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Make your phone number impossible to miss:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Place it prominently in the top right corner of every page</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Include it in the body copy of key pages</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Add it to a floating element that follows users as they scroll</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Ensure it appears multiple times throughout your site</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Take Action</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Log into your <a href="https://therebelape.com/services/website-design/">website builder</a> or contact your web developer today</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Add your phone number to the header on every page</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Make sure the font is large enough to be clearly visible</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>For bonus points, add a "Call Now" button next to the number</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Step 2: Ditch the Stock Photos—Use REAL Images of Your Team</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Problem</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Too many service businesses rely on generic stock photos that customers can spot from a mile away. This often leads to low conversion and when your plumbing website conversion rate is low, you’re losing money.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Why It Matters</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In today's world of AI-generated images and cookie-cutter websites, authenticity matters more than ever. Stock photos create an immediate disconnect between your business and potential customers.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>People want to do business with real people, not faceless companies. They want to know who's coming into their home, what your team looks like, and get a sense of your company culture before they pick up the phone.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This is especially critical now with the rise of AI-generated content. Just as people could spot stock photos before, they can now detect AI-generated images even faster. Authenticity has become a competitive advantage.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Solution</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Showcase the real faces behind your business:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Feature photos of your actual team members</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Show your technicians performing repairs</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Include images of your branded trucks and equipment</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Capture candid moments that highlight your company culture</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Take Action</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Schedule a team photoshoot (it doesn't need to be fancy!)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Take a group photo of your entire team standing in front of your branded trucks</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Feature this prominently on your homepage with a headline introducing your team</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Add individual photos of technicians to your "About Us" page and other various areas where you currently use stock photography</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Step 3: Add a Call-to-Action (CTA) After EVERY Block of Text</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Problem</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Many service business websites provide information but fail to tell visitors what to do next.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Why It Matters</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even when potential customers are interested in your services, they often need a clear prompt to take the next step. Without explicit direction, they may read your content, find it helpful, but then leave without contacting you.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Every section of your website should guide visitors toward becoming customers. If you're describing your drain cleaning service without immediately offering a way to book it, you're missing conversion opportunities.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Solution</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Never leave your visitors wondering what to do next:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>After every significant block of text, include a clear call-to-action</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Use action-oriented language like "Schedule Today," "Book Now," or "Call Us"</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Create a sense of urgency with words like "today" and "now"</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Make your CTAs visually distinct so they stand out from the rest of your content</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Take Action</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Review each page of your website and identify text blocks that describe services</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Add a CTA button after each of these sections</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Use consistent, action-oriented language across all CTAs</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Test different colors and styles to find what stands out best</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Step 4: Offer Chat or Two-Way Texting Options</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Problem</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Many service business owners resist adding chat features because they don't want to be glued to their phones answering questions all day.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Why It Matters</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not every potential customer wants to make a phone call. Some prefer the convenience of texting or chatting, especially for initial inquiries or when they're at work and can't talk.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I hear the objection frequently: "I'm already doing repairs, managing my company, and wearing multiple hats. I don't have time to sit there responding to chat messages all day."</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But here's the thing—you don't have to personally respond to every chat message. And by not offering this option, you're turning away potential customers who prefer this communication method.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Solution</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Implement chat or texting without creating more work for yourself:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Use a service like Podium or ServiceTitan that can handle initial inquiries</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Hire a virtual assistant to manage chat communications</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Set up a system where administrative staff can respond</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Some business owners even have their teenagers handle initial chat inquiries (where legally permitted)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The key is to have someone filter out the tire-kickers and only bring you into conversations with serious, qualified leads.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Take Action</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Research chat providers that integrate with your website</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Set up automated responses for common questions</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Establish a protocol for who handles chat inquiries and when they should involve you</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Add a chat widget to your website that's visible on all pages</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Step 5: Your Website Must Be Mobile-First, Not Just Mobile-Friendly</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Problem</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Many service business websites are designed primarily for desktop users, with mobile as an afterthought.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Why It Matters</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Google now indexes the mobile version of your website first. More importantly, at least 80% of initial visits to home service websites come from mobile devices.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Think about it—when someone has a plumbing emergency, they're not going to boot up their laptop. They're grabbing their phone, searching for "emergency plumber near me," and quickly jumping from site to site until they find someone to call.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If your website isn't optimized for mobile users, you're creating friction at the exact moment when customers need a seamless experience.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Solution</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Prioritize mobile users in your website design:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Ensure your site loads quickly on mobile devices</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Make your phone number tap-to-call on mobile</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Keep navigation simple and thumb-friendly</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Test your site on multiple devices and screen sizes</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The tap-to-call feature is particularly crucial—it allows potential customers to call you with a single tap rather than having to memorize your number and manually dial it.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Take Action</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Test your website on various mobile devices</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Ensure your phone number is clickable on mobile</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Simplify navigation for thumb-based browsing</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Consider adding a "Call Now" button that sticks to the bottom of the screen as users scroll</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Step 6: Your Site Must Load FAST</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Problem</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Slow-loading websites are conversion killers, especially for service businesses handling emergency situations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Why It Matters</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If your website takes more than 3-5 seconds to load, you're losing potential customers. Period. According to Google, 53% of mobile site visitors leave a page that takes longer than three seconds to load (<a href="https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/app-and-mobile/mobile-page-speed-new-industry-benchmarks/">source</a>). In emergency situations, every second counts, and customers won't wait for your beautiful but slow website to display.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>They'll simply hit the back button and try the next company in their search results. This is especially true for mobile users with varying connection speeds.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>The Solution</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Optimize your website for speed:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Compress images without sacrificing quality</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Minimize unnecessary plugins and scripts</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Use a content delivery network (CDN)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Implement browser caching</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As a bonus tip, set up automated text responses for missed calls. This allows you to re-engage potential customers who couldn't reach you immediately.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a></a>Take Action</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Test your website speed using&nbsp;<a href="https://pagespeed.web.dev/">Google PageSpeed Insights</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://developer.chrome.com/docs/lighthouse/">Lighthouse</a>, or&nbsp;<a href="https://gtmetrix.com/">GTmetrix</a>&nbsp;(my personal favorite for ease of use)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Identify and fix the elements slowing down your site, such as improper code, unoptimized images, excessive plugins, and render-blocking resources</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Work with your web developer to implement speed optimizations</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Set up automated text responses for missed calls</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->
