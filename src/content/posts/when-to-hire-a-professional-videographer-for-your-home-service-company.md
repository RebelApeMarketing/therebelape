## When to Hire a Professional Videographer: Equipment Considerations

Modern smartphones can capture impressive video for certain marketing purposes like social media updates and behind-the-scenes content. However, for brand videos, commercials, and cornerstone content, professional equipment makes a significant difference.

Of course, you can buy professional equipment yourself, but you should consider the cost of even the most basic professional setup. As you'll find, production can get expensive very quickly. There's no guarantee that you'll make enough money to cover the cost of the equipment, even if you manage to drive traffic to your product.

Professional videographers have a passion for their work and they purchase the right equipment to back it up:

- A professional videographer has the equipment and knowledge to make your product stand out. This means you don't have to start over whenever a take doesn't go as planned.
- Instead of spending a lot of cash on equipment that might not work for you, a videographer knows which tools to use. Plus it will only cost a fraction of the price of purchasing the equipment on your own.
- There are a wide range of video tools out there. You don't have to understand them all, only to realize that you got the wrong lens. Videographers know what they need to get the desired effect already.

Instead of spending precious money on equipment that you might not use, call in a video expert for your high-stakes content. You can get your money's worth without breaking the bank.

## When Your Smartphone is the Right Tool

For certain video types, your phone is not just adequate but often preferable:

- Quick social media updates and stories
- Time-sensitive content that needs to be published quickly
- Behind-the-scenes glimpses that benefit from an authentic feel
- Regular, high-volume content like tips and quick announcements
- When your budget is better allocated to content frequency rather than production value

The key is using your smartphone strategically and optimizing its capabilities:

- Invest in a simple external microphone for vastly improved audio
- Use natural lighting or inexpensive ring lights
- Keep shots stable with a basic tripod or gimbal

## The Strategic Approach to Video Marketing

The most successful approach combines both professional and smartphone videos:

- **Professional production** for cornerstone content like brand videos, commercials, and website features
- **Smartphone videos** for regular social media content, quick updates, and behind-the-scenes glimpses

For [home service businesses](/services/website-design/), this balanced approach allows you to:

- Create high-quality brand impressions with professional videos
- Stay consistently engaged with your audience through regular smartphone content
- Build a comprehensive video presence without breaking your budget

### When to Hire a Professional Videographer: Key Decision Points

*When to hire a professional videographer* often comes down to these critical factors:

- The video will be featured prominently on your website homepage
- You're creating a television commercial or major advertising campaign
- The content represents your overall brand identity and values
- The video will be used in sales presentations to secure high-value clients
